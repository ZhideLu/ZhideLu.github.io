About Me

I am currently a Researcher at the Shanghai Qi Zhi Institute. I received my Ph.D. from the Center for Quantum Information at IIIS, Tsinghua University. My research focuses on quantum error correction, driven by the ultimate goal of realizing practical, large-scale fault-tolerant quantum computation.

Research direction

  • Low-overhead quantum error correction: Design and experimental demonstration of quantum low-density parity-check (qLDPC) codes for high-threshold error suppression with reduced physical overhead.
  • Hardware-software co-design: Co-optimization of code architecture, topological mapping, and long-range coupler layout on superconducting processors to enable practical implementations of advanced QEC schemes.
  • Real-time decoding and feedback: Development of high-throughput, low-latency decoders and FPGA-based control systems for microsecond-scale closed-loop quantum error correction.
  • Fault-tolerant logical operations: Resource-efficient implementations of logical gates, lattice surgery, and magic state distillation tailored to high-rate qLDPC codes.
